Travelling to Leuwi Tonjong

  • Hervin Fakhrul
  • Travel
  • 14

Leuwi in Sundanese means lake. The green water in the lake in Cihurip District is being hunted by tourists. How not interested. on one side of the cliff, partially covered with plants is visible. There are two cliffs and in the middle there is a gap. From the gap, you can see a fairly small waterfall. Well, this is the beauty of Leuwi Tonjong. Enjoy the lake with its greenery on a raft. Tourists can also swim in this lake because it is not too deep. Actually, it's not too wide, but the presence of two cliffs covered in green plants makes Leuwi Tonjong have its own charm. Located in the southern region of Garut, so the direction to choose is to Pameungpeuk Beach. Arriving at Cihurip Village, after parking the vehicle, you still have to trek for 15-20 minutes. When from downtown Garut, about 3 hours. It's better if you visit this lake during the dry season, so you can find clear and greenish water. In the rainy season, the water tends to turn brown. The Garut Regency Government, West Java, began to fix the new Leuwi Tonjong tourist destinations in Cihurip District in the 2019 budget year with the start of building environmental road infrastructure around tourism. "In 2019 there will be a budget for environmental roads, hopefully 2019 will be completed," said Deputy Regent of Garut, Helmi Budiman in Garut, Thursday, December 6, 2018.

Leuwi Tonjong is the most beautiful place to relax in Garut, West Java.

He said, Leuwi Tonjong is a remote area in Garut which has a tourist attraction in the form of water flows and large rocks with natural surroundings that are still beautiful. In fact, the tour, he said, has been well known, and has even received an award from the Ministry of Tourism as a hidden paradise category tour. "The place is indeed good, and indeed hidden, that's why it's called the Hidden Heaven," said Helmi. According to him, the Leuwi Tonjong tourist location has a very exotic place, so the facilities and infrastructure need to be encouraged so that visitors are comfortable and safe during their tour. He hopes that hidden tours can be easily reached by vehicles, so that everyone can enjoy them. "I see how we can enjoy this hidden thing, and currently the problems with access, parking, arrangement at the location are still minimal," he said.
